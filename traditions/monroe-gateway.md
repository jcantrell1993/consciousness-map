## The Focus Levels

Monroe's framework maps states of consciousness numerically. The lower numbers are closer to ordinary waking awareness; the higher numbers move progressively deeper.

**Focus 10** — "Mind Awake / Body Asleep." The body enters sleep while conscious awareness remains. This is the threshold state where the vibrational state often appears. The gateway to everything beyond.

**Focus 12** — Expanded awareness. Sensory noise drops. Perception extends beyond the physical body's immediate environment. A state of receptivity.

**Focus 15** — "No Time." Ordinary temporal sequencing begins to dissolve. Past and future become accessible as fields rather than sequences.

**Focus 21** — The edge of the physical matter energy system. The threshold where contact with non-physical intelligences becomes possible. Monroe described this as the "bridge" state.

**Focus 22–27** — Deeper non-physical territories. The Belief System Territories (Focus 23–25), the Reception Center (Focus 27). These are Monroe's maps of post-physical states — not necessarily doctrine but useful landmarks.

## The Vibrational State

Monroe's detailed description: a sensation of energy or vibration rising through the body, often starting in the chest or solar plexus, sometimes accompanied by a sense of pressure or buzzing. It is the transition signal — the body entering the dissociated state necessary for OBE. It can be frightening the first time. The correct response is not to resist it but to allow it to intensify and stabilize.

Key practice note: *don't grab at it.* The vibrational state is delicate at threshold. Excitement or effort collapses it back. Passive allowing is the technique.

---

## OBE Mechanics (Monroe's Account)

The separation is typically gradual. Monroe described multiple methods — rolling out, floating upward, using a rope visualization, simply intending movement. The moment of separation is often marked by a snap or click sensation, or by the sudden shift to visual awareness of the room from a different perspective.

The common failure point Jacob has encountered — reaching the edge of separation and having it collapse — is well-documented. Monroe's advice: at the threshold, *wait longer than feels necessary before trying to move.* The state needs to stabilize fully before locomotion is attempted.
