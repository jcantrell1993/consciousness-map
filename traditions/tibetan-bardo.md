# Tibetan Book of the Dead & Dream Yoga

*Working reference — held lightly as a map made by other travelers, not as doctrine.*

---

## The Core Framework

The Tibetan tradition offers perhaps the most detailed cartography of non-ordinary consciousness states in any contemplative tradition. Two related bodies of practice are relevant here:

**The Bardo Thodol (Liberation Through Hearing in the Between)** — A text designed to be read to the dying, guiding them through the stages of dissolution and the intermediate states between death and rebirth. But its map of consciousness states is equally applicable to deep meditation, sleep, and altered states encountered in life.

**Dream Yoga (Milam)** — One of the Six Yogas of Naropa. The practice of maintaining awareness through the sleep and dream states, ultimately recognizing all experience — including the dream — as the display of mind's own nature.

---

## The Bardos (Intermediate States)

The tradition describes several bardos, of which three are most relevant:

**Kyenay Bardo** — The bardo of waking life. Ordinary consciousness. The training ground.

**Milam Bardo** — The bardo of dream. The practice of lucid dreaming as preparation for the deeper bardos. If you can recognize the dream as dream, you are practicing recognition in the state that most closely resembles the after-death experience.

**Chikhai Bardo** — The bardo of dying. The moment of dissolution of ordinary consciousness. The tradition teaches that a brilliant, luminous awareness — the Clear Light of the Dharmata — appears at this moment. Recognition of it as one's own nature is liberation. Most people, unprepared, faint before it.

**Chonyid Bardo** — Peaceful and wrathful deities appear as projections of mind. The tradition holds these as luminous displays of awareness's own energy, not external entities.

**Sidpa Bardo** — The bardo of becoming, leading toward rebirth.

---

## The Clear Light

This is the central concept. At the deepest level of any dissolution — death, deep dreamless sleep, the peak of certain meditative states — there is said to be a luminous, naked awareness that is the ground of all experience. It is not a thing seen but the seeing itself.

The purple light Jacob encountered at the hypnagogic threshold may be a less-refined version of threshold luminosity. The tradition catalogues a range of light phenomena at different depths.

---

## Sleep Yoga Stages

The practice sequence in dream yoga:
1. Maintain awareness at the hypnagogic threshold (the point Jacob is working with)
2. Recognize the dream state as dream
3. Navigate and stabilize within the dream
4. Ultimately, rest in the dreamless sleep state — the luminous ground

The hypnagogic threshold is exactly the gateway both Monroe and the Tibetan tradition agree on. Monroe's Focus 10 and the first stage of Milam practice describe the same territory from different angles.
